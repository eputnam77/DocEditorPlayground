2. Applicability

The guidance provided in this AC is for manufacturers, modifiers, foreign regulatory authorities, and FAA engineers, and their designees.

The contents of this AC do not have the force and effect of law and are not meant to bind the public in any way. This AC is intended only to provide information to the public regarding existing requirements under the law or agency policies. Conformity with the guidance is voluntary only and nonconformity will not affect rights and obligations under existing statutes and regulations. The FAA will consider other methods of demonstrating compliance that an applicant may elect to present. Terms such as “should,” “may,” and “must” are used only in the sense of ensuring applicability of this particular method of compliance when the acceptable method of compliance in this document is used. If the FAA becomes aware of circumstances in which following this AC would not result in compliance with the applicable regulations, the FAA may require additional substantiation as the basis for finding compliance.

This material does not change or create any additional regulatory requirements or authorize changes in or permit deviations from existing regulatory requirements.

8. Table of Contents

You are not required to have a table of contents (TOC) in a short AC, but you may add one if it will help the reader navigate the document. Using this template allows you to easily auto-generate one. See Microsoft Word Help for guidance on auto-generating a TOC.

9. Tables/Figures

There are two styles available for formatting a table/figure: “Caption” and “Caption Image (for Figure).” You can use the “Caption” style to format the table/figure number and heading. Place the caption above the table/figure. You can use the “Caption Image (for Figure)” style to format the paragraph alignment behind a figure/picture.

10. Variables

This template contains four predefined character styles that you can use to create subscript or superscript text, which you might typically use when referring to a variable in an equation: “Variable Subscript,” “Variable Subscript + Bold,” “Variable Subscript + Italic,” and “Variable Superscript.”
